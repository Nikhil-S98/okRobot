Generative AI and Postmodernism

Generative AI and simulacra are closely related to postmodernism due to their shared themes and implications.


Postmodernism challenges the idea of a singular, objective reality and emphasizes the subjective nature of truth and meaning. It suggests that reality is constructed through language, culture, and various forms of representation. Similarly, generative AI and simulacra operate in realms where reality is constructed or simulated rather than objectively observed.


Generative AI, a transformative technology, creates new content—text, images, or music—based on patterns learned from existing data. This process, mirroring human creativity, raises profound questions about authorship, originality, and authenticity. In a postmodern context, the output of generative AI blurs the distinction between the "real" and the "simulated," challenging traditional notions of creativity and representation.


Simulacra, a concept popularized by French philosopher Jean Baudrillard, refers to copies of things that have lost their original meaning or reference point. In a world saturated with images, symbols, and simulations, simulacra replaces reality with representations that may bear little resemblance to the original. This notion resonates with the outputs of generative AI, which can produce convincing imitations of human-generated content without any inherent understanding of its meaning.

AI in Media

Media such as Wachowskis' The Matrix embodies this notion that reality is constructed in relation to a mirror that produces what we want to see. In the library space, we see implementations of generative AI as a step toward information retrieval production, with a slight slope toward reliance on artificial intelligence/interaction.


Additionally, William Gibson's Neuromancer produced a cyber world during a time when the Internet was limited. His world-building influenced a speculative notion of technology aids for interaction, such as cell phones, online messaging, and omnipresent surveillance. The reflection of technology back to us heightens the reality that we are subjected to our fear, and we reiterate copies of a copy of a copy of information that transforms into fragmented reality.


Moreover, both generative AI and simulacra highlight the role of technology in shaping our perceptions of reality. They demonstrate how advancements in technology allow us to create and manipulate representations of reality, blurring the boundaries between the real and the simulated.


In essence, generative AI and simulacra are manifestations of postmodern themes such as the fragmentation of reality, the proliferation of representations, and the dissolution of traditional categories. They invite us to reconsider our assumptions about creativity, authenticity, and the nature of reality in an increasingly mediated world.
